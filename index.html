Operating Doctrine

Standing guidance governing prototype lifecycle and resource allocation.

15-Hour Prototyping Limit

No prototype may consume more than fifteen (15) hours of build time without demonstrating operational value. If a prototype cannot show a viable capability within that window, it is terminated and recorded in the Graveyard. The objective is speed to evidence — not finished polish.

14-Day Bingo Fuel Timeline

Every operation begins with a full allocation. The T-Clock counts upward from the launch date in working days — weekends and published holidays are excluded — and fuel state advances automatically:

  • T+0 to T+10 — Nominal. Active development window. Build, test, deploy.
  • T+11 to T+13 — Joker. Warning state. Commit to delivery or stand down.
  • T+14 and beyond — Bingo. Critical. Promote to Core or retire to the Graveyard.

Resources are finite. An operation either earns promotion or releases its allocation.

      The Three Gates

      These gates are non-negotiable. They enforce the doctrine — only fast, high-leverage, data-ready work reaches the tracker. If a candidate fails any single gate, it does not launch.

      01

      Data Ready?

      The data this operation depends on already exists and is reachable today.

      Clears You can pull the data now (API, database, export, files) and it is clean enough to prototype against.

      Fails You would first have to build a pipeline, secure new approvals, or spend weeks cleaning a dataset.

      02

      Under 15 Hours?

      A working prototype can be stood up within the 15-hour prototyping limit.

      Clears You can demonstrate the core capability in 15 hours of build time or less.

      Fails It needs deep integration, heavy infrastructure, or weeks of engineering before anything works.

      03

      2+ Hr ROI?

      The operation returns at least two hours of value every cycle.

      Clears It saves 2+ hours per run, user, or week — and you can state the number.

      Fails The payoff is marginal, vague, or cannot be quantified.
